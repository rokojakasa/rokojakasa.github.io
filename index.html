Project Overview

What we set out to find

San Francisco's publicly available incident reports give a rare window into where, when, and how crime clusters across the city. This project combines probability analysis, temporal heatmaps, and district-level mapping to surface patterns that are difficult to see in raw tables.

Three static charts establish the city-wide picture; four interactive visualisations then let you drill into specific days, hours, and neighbourhoods at your own pace.

Key Takeaways

What the data tells us

Crime in San Francisco is deeply shaped by geography and time. Certain districts carry disproportionate shares of specific incident types, while temporal analysis shows clear peaks tied to weekend nights and late hours. Probability-normalised views reveal patterns invisible in raw counts — and interactive exploration turns those patterns into something you can actually feel in the data.
